Sirloin Steak With Garlic Butter

Home

Description

This sirloin steak recipe is served with very garlicky butter that makes this steak melt-in-your-mouth wonderful! I have never tasted any other steak that came even close to this recipe!

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup butter
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 teaspoons garlic powder
  • 4 pounds beef sirloin steaks
  • salt and pepper to taste

Directions

  1. Gather all ingredients. Preheat an outdoor grill for high heat and lightly oil the grate.
  2. Melt butter in a small saucepan over medium-low heat.
  3. Bring a large pot of lightly salted water to a boil. Cook spaghetti in boiling water, stirring occasionally, until tender yet slightly firm to the bite, about 12 minutes.
  4. Stir in minced garlic and garlic powder. Set aside.
  5. Season both sides of each steak with salt and pepper.
  6. Place steaks on preheated grill and cook 4 to 5 minutes per side. An instant-read thermometer inserted into the center should read 140 degrees F (60 degrees C) for medium doneness.
  7. Transfer steaks to warmed plates; brush the tops liberally with garlic butter and allow to rest for 2 to 3 minutes before serving.
